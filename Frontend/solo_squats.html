Squat Counter

This code creates a squat counter web page that uses your computer camera to monitor your exercise form and count the number of squats you perform.

When you click the start button, the page asks for access to your camera and begins displaying a hidden video feed (used for analysis) and a canvas

where it draws the video frames. It then loads a machine learning model that detects key parts of your body (like your hips, knees, and ankles) and

continuously calculates the angle at your knee. When the model sees that you squat down (the knee angle becomes small) and then stand back up (the knee

angle becomes large again), it increments your squat counter and plays a beep sound. The code also draws markers on your video image to help visualize the

detected body points, making it easier to see how the model is tracking your movements.
